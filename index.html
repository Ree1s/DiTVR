Abstract

Video restoration aims to reconstruct high-quality video sequences from low-quality inputs, addressing tasks such as super-resolution, denoising, and deblurring. Traditional regression-based methods often produce unrealistic details and require extensive paired datasets, while generative diffusion models face challenges in ensuring temporal consistency. To overcome these limitations, we introduce DiTVR, a novel zero-shot video restoration framework that leverages a pre-trained Diffusion Transformer operating directly in pixel space. Unlike prior methods that utilize optical flow solely for structural guidance, DiTVR integrates a spatiotemporal neighbor cache, trajectory-aware attention, and a flow-guided diffusion samplerâ€”all driven by optical flow trajectories extracted from low-quality inputs. These components enhance spatial alignment and robust temporal coherence across frames. Additionally, our flow-guided diffusion sampler refines neglected detail improvement during inference, ensuring temporal alignment and realistic detail simultaneously. Extensive experiments show that DiTVR achieves state-of-the-art performance on zero-shot video restoration benchmarks, excelling in temporal consistency and high-fidelity restoration without task-specific training.
